Understanding Clusters & Sub-Clusters in Campaign Planning

To make OOH planning more accurate and data-driven, our platform uses AI-powered analysis to organize every state into intelligent geographic zones called Clusters and Sub-Clusters. These zones help you understand where audiences concentrate, how movement happens, and which areas deliver the highest advertising impact.

This structure powers your reach, impressions, frequency, and audience insights — so you can choose locations based on performance, not guesswork.

What Is a Cluster?

A Cluster is a large activity zone within a state where people, businesses, and movement patterns naturally concentrate.

Each cluster represents an area with similar:

  • Audience Activity Levels

  • Traffic Intensity

  • Population Density

  • Exposure Potential for OOH Media

Clusters allow you to compare different parts of a city or state based on how valuable they are for advertising.

What Is a Sub-Cluster?

A Sub-Cluster is a more detailed zone inside a cluster.

It represents a smaller, more precise area where activity patterns are highly consistent — such as business districts, transit corridors, shopping belts, or mixed-use neighborhoods.

Each sub-cluster includes:

• Reach – Share of the total audience exposure

• Impressions – Daily, weekly, and monthly visibility estimates

• Frequency – How often an average person is likely to see your ad (Frequency ≈ 1 means at least one expected exposure)

• Population Density – How concentrated people and activity are

• Audience Personas – Dominant behavioral audience types in the area

This allows you to plan at both a strategic level (clusters) and a precision level (sub-clusters).

How Our Platform Creates Clusters & Sub-Clusters

Our clustering is powered by AI and data science.

We analyze:

  • Points of Interest (POIs)such as shopping areas, offices, schools, transport hubs, entertainment zones, etc

  • Traffic Patternshow movement flows across different areas

  • Audience Behavior & Personasinferred from surrounding activity

  • Geographic Relationshipshow locations influence one another spatially

Using our AI-powered modeling system, we group nearby high-activity areas together and calculate performance metrics for each zone.

This creates a consistent, comparable structure across the entire country.

Overall Clustering vs. Planning-Time Clustering

There are two layers of clustering on our platform:

1. Platform-Level Clustering (Baseline)

This is our default intelligence layer.

For every state, we maintain a continuously refined cluster and sub-cluster structure that represents:

• Typical Movement Patterns

• Long-Term Audience Behavior

• Average Traffic Intensity

• Standard Reach and Impression Distributions

This ensures every media location uploaded to the platform can be evaluated using a consistent performance framework.

2. Planning-Module Clustering (Dynamic & Personalized)

When you start planning a campaign, clustering becomes adaptive.

Based on your inputs such as:

• Target POIs

• Selected Audience Personas

• Campaign Timing

• Budget and Scale

Our system runs real-time clustering scripts using our AI-powered engine to:

• Re-Evaluate Activity Zones

• Recalculate Reach, Impressions, and Frequency

• Re-Prioritize areas based on your campaign objectives

This means:

Two users planning in the same state can see different cluster structures — optimized specifically for their campaign goals.

You are not choosing from static zones, but from AI-optimized opportunity areas tailored to your strategy.

Why This Matters for Your Campaign

Clustering helps you:

• Identify high-impact zones instantly

• Compare areas using consistent performance metrics

• Target locations that match your audience behavior

• Avoid low-efficiency placements

• Allocate budget where visibility and exposure are strongest

• Instead of selecting sites one-by-one blindly, you plan using intelligent geographic performance zones.

In Simple Terms

• Clusters = Big opportunity zones

• Sub-clusters = Precise high-value pockets

• Metrics = Reach, impressions, frequency, population density, audience type

• Planning Module = AI recalculates everything based on your campaign needs

All the complexity runs in the background — so you can focus on choosing the areas that deliver results.
